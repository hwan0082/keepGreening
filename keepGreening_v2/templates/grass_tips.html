Tips for growing grass

Environmentally friendly lawn care

To reduce the environmental impact of my lawn, you can use renewable energy to power an electric mower and edger. Electric mowers are quieter than fuel ones and, when using clean energy, they eliminate pollution. Use the electric mower when the ground is soft, otherwise mow the lawn with an old hand-propelled mower.

How to choose the right lawn

Successful lawns are a product of the right location, right type of grass and methodical maintenance. Like any crop, turf needs cultivation. The best lawns are established from scratch, and it's easier and preferable to replace a poor lawn than to resurrect one.Good maintenance involves paying as much attention to the condition of the soil as to the turf itself. Turf is superior to seed mixes. Most mixes require cool conditions and regular watering.

Best types of grass for Australian gardens

Choosing the right lawn type for your garden involves considering a wide range of factors, including the amount of sunlight exposure, weather conditions and your own tendencies as a gardener. The six most reliable turf grasses for Australian gardens are: Common couch, Queensland blue, Durban, Kikuyu, Sir Walter buffalo, and Saltine turf (or saltwater couch), as well as their cultivars.

How to maintain your lawn

For a successful lawn, you need a regime of mowing, feeding and aerating. Cut at the highest setting your mower permits all year round. This leafy growth helps the lawn to smother and exclude weeds. Nourish your lawn by feeding it with granular, organic poultry manure once every three months during the growing season. Apply it just before rain. Spray with seaweed tonic once a month. As well as feeding, ensure the soil pH remains between 6 and 7. Use a pH test kit to check soil every autumn, which is also the best time to condition soil. Use a fork, or mechanical aerator, to break up soil compaction. Aerate in spring and again in autumn to help water penetration and encourage strong root growth.

How to establish a new lawn

Here are some key points that will help you establish a lawn successfully. Choose or create a fairly level site, where water can soak in, not run off. Turf needs at least three hours of sunshine per day in winter, and more in summer, so it can grow thick and strong and win out over weeds.

1. Prepare the soil for grass

Prepare the soil well by digging it thoroughly and working in plenty of compost or well-rotted animal manures. If necessary, apply gypsum to improve and break up heavy clay.

2. Adjust soil's ph level

Adjust the soil's pH if needed. Turf grasses grow best in soil conditions that are neutral to slightly acidic, with a pH between 6 and 7.

4. Lay the turf
